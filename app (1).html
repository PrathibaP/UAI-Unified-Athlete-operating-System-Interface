UAI — the athlete's operating system.

08 · Business Rules

Trust is earned, not declared.

A Trust Score below 40 hides sponsor visibility until re-verification. Funding requests above ₹50,000 require dual approval from admin and a finance reviewer. Sponsorship terms cannot be edited after an athlete has accepted — only superseded by a new offer with full audit history.

09 · Sponsorship Logic

Visibility is a function of trust, not popularity.

Sponsors see a ranked feed weighted by trust score, performance percentile, sport-category fit, and prior fulfillment record — never by follower count. Every match surfaces its reasoning so sponsors can audit why an athlete was recommended.

10 · Admin Workflow

Federation-grade oversight, without the bottleneck.

Admins review flagged verifications, approve high-value disbursements, and manage federation-level rosters — with every action logged to an immutable audit trail visible to compliance reviewers.

Verification data flow — how a single Aadhaar + bank-link submission turns into a higher Trust Score:

Athlete submits Aadhaar number from the Verify Identity flow; request hits the API Gateway with device + consent metadata.
Identity service calls UIDAI eKYC, returns OTP challenge; on success it triggers a liveness face-match step.
Bank account is linked and validated with a ₹1 penny-drop reconciliation against the IFSC/account pair.
An event is published to the event bus; Trust Score Engine recalculates the composite score in real time.
Encrypted records are persisted (Postgres for structured data, S3 for scans), and the athlete's profile, sponsor visibility, and funding eligibility update instantly.
